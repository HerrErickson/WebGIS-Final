Origins of the New York EJ Screen

The EPA's EJ Screen is a mapping tool dedicated to displaying the environmental and social indices of every community in the United States. At Region 2, we strive to serve the States and Terriroties of New York, New Jersey, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands. One of the attributes counts the number of indices exceeded by every comunity, making it the most important field to dislay. Thus, we created a basemap of the environmental and social indices for the individual states in our region, in order to focus our work on communities that need it most. The map serves to locate current and future efforts in regard to who needs it most.
Because of the dashboard containing much data, this website is divided into two dashboards to avoid cluter and focus on different aspects of the EJ Screen.

Statistical View

This Dashboard serves to provide a look into the numbers behind environmental justice indices in New York State. Included within the dashboard are two indicators. The indicator in the top left corner depicts the count of average indices exceeded in an area, changing with zoom. The indicator in the bottom left depicts the average count of all exceeded indices and how many communities exceed the average. Lastly, the chart on the bottom right depicts the distribution indices exceeded.

Broad Index View

The EJ Screen is much more than counts exceeded, offering over 180 different indices. However, these 180 indices are acrynoms that are unknown to the public. Thus we paired the map with a list of the acrynoms and their full names. This dashboard serves to offer an easy view of all the different indices acorss New York's communities
